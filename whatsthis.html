What's This?

Why Read This Blog?

This blog offers unconventional perspectives that challenge established thinking patterns. If you're looking for new ways to approach persistent problems or fresh insights on familiar topics, you'll find value here.

What You'll Gain

Reading these essays will help you:

  • Break free from binary thinking - Learn techniques like dimensional decoupling that separate concepts typically viewed as inseparable
  • Develop mental flexibility - Discover how to hold multiple, sometimes contradictory perspectives simultaneously
  • Spot hidden opportunities - Recognize value in seemingly unimportant activities and counterintuitive approaches
  • Make better decisions - Navigate complex problems with nuanced frameworks rather than simplistic models

Key Concepts

You'll encounter several ideas here that you probably won't find articulated the same way elsewhere:

Dimensional Decoupling: While inversion asks "what if something was the opposite?", dimensional decoupling asks "what if something wasn't an opposite?" This perspective unlocks new possibilities by breaking artificial constraints. (Read more)

Preference Changing: Tired of fighting yourself? Rather than using willpower to resist what you want, learn techniques to change what you want in the first place. This approach eliminates the internal tug-of-war that makes most changes so exhausting. (Read more)

Loose Feedback Loops: Everyone says faster feedback is better, right? Not so fast. Tight feedback loops can trap you in local maxima and prevent breakthrough insights. They feel productive but often lead to optimization of the wrong things. The most important discoveries require stepping away from immediate validation and venturing into the unknown. (Read more)

The Importance of "Unimportant" Activities: Activities typically classified as unimportant or unproductive often create serendipity and opportunities that cannot be planned for. That supposedly wasteful time might be creating your most valuable future opportunities. (Read more)

Practical Applications

These concepts aren't merely theoretical. Readers have applied them to:

  • Change persistent habits without relying on willpower
  • Identify blind spots in their decision-making processes
  • Find creative solutions to problems that seemed unsolvable
  • Build more effective personal and organizational systems
  • Recognize patterns in seemingly unrelated domains

Who This Blog Is For

This blog is particularly valuable for:

  • Independent thinkers looking for ideas outside the mainstream
  • Problem-solvers stuck with challenges that standard approaches can't fix
  • People interested in mental models and thinking tools
  • Those looking to understand complex systems, from personal psychology to societal structures
  • Anyone who enjoys seeing familiar concepts from new angles
